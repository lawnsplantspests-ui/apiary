Local bee pressures

What Mechanicsburg properties typically call us about

Mechanicsburg is South Mountain foothills, with a mix of established neighborhoods, newer construction off Trindle Road, and rural-edge properties bordering woodlots. The typical bee-related calls we get from the area:

  • Honey bee swarms from late April through June, peaking in May — clusters often on residential maples and ornamental trees
  • Established honey bee colonies in older Mechanicsburg soffits, chimneys, and wall voids
  • Bumble bee nests in lawns, mulch piles, and under sheds — common from May through July
  • Yellow jacket and bald-faced hornet identification calls in late summer (handled by our sister LPP)
  • Carpenter bee damage on cedar fascia (sister LPP handles)

Not sure what you're seeing? Send a photo to 717-379-3248 — we identify honey bees vs. bumble bees vs. wasps vs. hornets for free, then route the call to whichever side of the family handles it.

Why call a beekeeper

We're not exterminators

When you call a pest control company about bees, the technician's job is to make the bees go away. When you call The Alleman Apiary, our job is to make the bees go home with us. We use bee vacuums that collect honey bees alive, save and reuse comb where possible, and the colony goes into one of our hives — not a trash bag.

If what you actually have is yellow jackets, hornets, or wasps, our sister business Lawns Plants & Pests LLC handles those at the same phone number, 717-379-3248. One call, two specialties, all stinging insects handled the right way.
